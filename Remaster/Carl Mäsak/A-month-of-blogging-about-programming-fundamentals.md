# A month of blogging about programming fundamentals
    
*Originally published on [31 May 2011](http://strangelyconsistent.org/blog/a-month-of-blogging-about-programming-fundamentals) by Carl Mäsak.*

I got it into my head to make a short-ish blog post every day during June, building up programming knowledge from the very basics into something that could be used for everyday programming.

The target audience: let's say a highschooler wanting to get into programming. Someone who doesn't care that it's called "conditional", but who will call it an if for a couple of years to come at least. But I imagine the posts might be enjoyable for other readers as well; not least those who want to get into Raku but to whom the synopses are just too information-dense and imposing.

I plan to introduce one, sometimes two, concepts a day and just show what you can do with them. Every five days or so I will write a slightly longer piece of code pulling together those concepts. Most often into a game or other. (I owe much of my computer interest to [this book](https://www.amazon.com/BASIC-Computer-Games-Microcomputer-David/dp/0894800523/), which I perused with great interest during high school.)

I only have half a plan so far, but that's OK. It looks like this:

- I/O (`say` and `prompt`)
- variables
- `if` statements
- simple loops
- **guess a number between 1 and 100**
- string concatenation and `x```
- arithmetic
- `substr```
- `given` statement
- **moon lander**
- arrays
- `for` loops
- `.push` et al
- `.comb```
- **chomp!**

Looking forward to a month of no-nonsense educational Raku blogging!
