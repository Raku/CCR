# Oslo Raku Hackathon Notes
    
*Originally published on [13 April 2009](https://use-perl.github.io/user/pmichaud/journal/38805/) by Patrick Michaud.*

As some of you are aware, this week is the 
<a class='urllink' href='http://www.rakuworkshop.no/npw2009/' rel='nofollow'>Nordic Perl Workshop</a>,
and in the days immediately following the workshop we will have the 
<a class='urllink' href='http://www.rakufoundation.org/raku/index.cgi?oslo_perl_6_hackaton_2009' rel='nofollow'>Oslo Raku Hackathon</a>.  During the first day of the hackathon
Gabor Szabo will be doing a 
<a class='urllink' href='http://szabgab.com/blog/2009/03/1235863222.html' rel='nofollow'>"Hands-on Raku training" course</a>,
the other two days will be for various hacking tasks (both Raku
and Perl).


I personally have three goals for my participation at the
workshop and hackathon:

-  Attend Gabor's course and take careful note of where issues arise with using Rakudo Perl and/or Raku (including filing bug reports and/or answering questions as needed).
<li> Recruit and encourage people to write Raku programs and otherwise start hacking on/with Rakudo Perl.
<li> Meet with other principal Rakudo and Raku designers and implementors to plan the next phases of work and address any significant obstacles currently before us.


For now I've started a page at
    <a class='urllink' href='http://www.rakufoundation.org/raku/index.cgi?perl_6_hackathon_targets' rel='nofollow'>http://www.rakufoundation.org/raku/index.cgi?perl_6_hackathon_targets</a>
that lists a variety of specific ideas for things to work on
or address at the hackathon.  I'm sure more ideas will arise
at NPW itself, and I invite others to add more items to the
list as well.  Feel free to add things even if you won't be
making it to NPW or the hackathon itself; you may think of something
that we've overlooked, and there are other hackathons planned later
in the summer that will benefit from having the ideas.


Thanks!


Pm
